Privacy Policy

Effective date: October 2025

The Woof of Walk Street respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose and safeguard your information when you interact with us.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information when you use our services:

  • Contact details (name, email address, phone number)
  • Residential address (for dog walking and pick-up services)
  • Payment information (processed securely via third-party providers)
  • Details about your pet (name, breed, health or behavioural notes)
  • Website usage information (via cookies, analytics tools)

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Provide and manage our dog walking and boarding services
  • Schedule bookings and communicate with you
  • Process payments
  • Send service updates, promotional offers (with your consent)
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations in Victoria and Australia

3. Disclosure of Information

We will not sell or rent your personal information. We may disclose it to:

  • Our staff, contractors and service providers to deliver services
  • Payment processors (e.g. Stripe, PayPal) who manage secure transactions
  • Regulatory bodies if required by law

4. Data Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorised access, modification or disclosure. All electronic data is stored securely on servers with restricted access.

5. Cookies and Analytics

Our website may use cookies and analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) to improve user experience and understand site traffic. You may disable cookies through your browser settings, though some site features may not function properly.

6. Direct Marketing

We may send you promotional material if you have opted in. You can unsubscribe at any time using the link in our emails or by contacting us directly.

7. Access and Correction

You may request access to the personal information we hold about you, or request corrections, by contacting us. We will respond within a reasonable timeframe in accordance with the APPs.

8. Cross-Border Disclosure

Some of our third-party service providers may store data outside of Australia. Where this occurs, we ensure that appropriate data protection measures are in place.

9. Complaints

If you believe we have breached the Australian Privacy Principles, you may contact us. We will respond promptly and work to resolve your concerns. If you are not satisfied, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).
